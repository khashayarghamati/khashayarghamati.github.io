About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Computer Science at the University of Hertfordshire, UK, and Principal AI Engineer at Inframatic. I specialise in crafting AI models, intelligent agents, and adaptive systems — with particular expertise in reinforcement learning, agentic AI, continual learning, and bio-inspired cognition applied to robotics and human-robot interaction.

My research has been published in leading venues including IEEE TCDS, IEEE RO-MAN, IEEE Humanoids, MDPI Sensors, and ICSR. I also co-organise the Special Session on Continual Learning and Generative AI in Social Robotics at ICSR 2026 and lecture in Computer Science at the University of Hertfordshire, collaborating internationally with the University of Luxembourg.

I am passionate about building AI that is adaptive, explainable, and grounded in real-world deployment — bridging the gap between biological intelligence and autonomous systems. Whether designing novel learning architectures, engineering production AI pipelines, or deploying robots in unstructured environments, I bring a hands-on, research-driven approach to solving complex problems.
