About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Hertfordshire, UK, focusing on agentic AI, continual learning, novelty detection, and reinforcement learning for adaptive robotics and human-robot interaction (HRI). My work bridges cutting-edge machine learning with real-world robotics—enabling intelligent systems to learn on the fly, adapt in unseen environments, and interact robustly with humans.

With hands-on experience as an AI engineer and lecturer, I am passionate about open, explainable, and trustworthy AI. My research aims to build future robots that continually improve, safely adapt, and enhance human lives through sophisticated cognitive architectures and adaptive learning mechanisms.

My work has been published in leading venues including IEEE RO-MAN, MDPI Sensors, IEEE Humanoids, and ICSR, with research contributions spanning continual learning frameworks, personalised language models, and cognitive plausibility studies that advance the field of human-centred AI.
